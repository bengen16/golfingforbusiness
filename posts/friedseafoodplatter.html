The Fried Seafood Platter

A fisherman's platter. A seafood sampler. A nautical trencher. Fried seafood platters go by many names. But regardless of what they're called, each one usually consists of a heaping pile of breaded and fried maritime critters, a wedge of lemon, and often a side of tartar. I love seafood platters. As someone with frequent dining-out-induced buyers remorse, a seafood platter offers variety. And even though fried seafood isn't really inherently historical in any way, it makes me feel like I'm eating what a 19th century working class New Englander ate which is, for some reason, a feeling I really want to experience.
